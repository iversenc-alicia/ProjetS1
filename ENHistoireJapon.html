Japan’s women history since 20th century

To realise the starting point of Japan’s feminism, it is important to realise that because of her biological attributes (birth, menstruation ...), women polarise a big number or filths. Withe filth (death) and red filth (blood). For a long time we have forbidden women to enter multiple places. They were considered too impure to enter in these places.

With Meiji’s Restoration in 1868, the Japan that we know nowadays as “the old Japan" changed. It became moremodern. It was a true blessing for women who began to fight for recognition. It was in this context that the feminism movementbegan in Japan and developed through the 20th century..

A difficult start

To begin with, it is important to understand that even if mouvements like the Tokyo Federation of Women’s Organisations (Tokyo Rengo Funjinkai)appeared, women are not considered equal to men. Women have no voice, there is what is called the 3 submission ruleA woman is submissive to her father, husband and son. She has to obey any man in her family. We could even wonder if women are even considered as human beings at all.
To illustrate this, it is possible to talk about “comfort women”.This is a softened expression for the sexual slaverysystem made for the Japanese Army. Indeed, after the Nanking massacre in 1937, the government created the system of comfort women . The goal was to limit STI (sexually transmissible infections) provoked by the many rapes and keep the mood of the soldiers up. They organised roundups to take women by force from their homes. Women were taken into “comfort houses” in order to satisfy the sexual desires of the Japan imperial army’s soldiers.

Specialists said that 200 000 Korean, Chinese and Japanese women were victims of this horrible system. In 2013, the Mayor of Osaka, Touru Hashimoto, said that“the comfort women system was a necessity.”. Hearing this, it is not even possible to speak about equality between genders. Women are considered as objects that are used without their consent. .

Women’s quiet revolution

Between the years 1950 and 1970a sexist family model appeared. Paradoxically, this is what allowed women to affirm themselves. There was a sexual division of the roles. The shufu (stay at home mother) ideology with the idea that each gender has a particular competence makes its appearance. So women who have never been listened to until now are starting to be heard. The woman is still not considered as the equal of the man, but as a person who completes him. She is trusted with real tasks like raising the children while the man works. This is a step into education for women because it is necessary to be able to educate their children.

Since the husband works most of the time, the woman gets used to her husband's absence. She becomes more and more independent. As a consequence, after a few years, the dominant model changed from sengyô shufu "full-time housewife" to kenguû shufu "working housewife".

Women’s situation nowadays

Even nowadays, the situation of women is still complicated in Japan. Indeed, the mentalities in Japan are not made for movements like #MeToo. Many people consider it too aggressive for Japan. According to an article in L'Express , inequalities are that strong at work and at home that many women think there is no way out and #metoo is useless. Even today, women still spend many more hours than men on household tasks. For example, we can see in the book presented in the article “ the situation of women in numbers ” that in a study done between 2012 and 2018, women spend on average 3.6 hours doing domestic tasks compared to less than one hour for men.

Important dates in the evolution of women's condition in Japan

1911-1916

Seitô: first feminist literary revue created by women.

1922

Women were allowed to attend political assemblies, but were not allowed to participate in political parties or vote.

1923

Establishment of the Tokyo Women's Organizations Federation (Tokyo Rengo Funjinkai)

1945-1952

After the defeat of the Second World War, the US military occupation led by the General Mac Arthur forced Japan to write a new Constitution.

1945

Right to vote for women

1954

Women organized a campaign against defective matchsticks and were successful.

Années 1970

beginning of the fall of arranged marriages ( presently around 6%).

1986

Kintô-hô: law on gender equality at work. Before this law, gender inequality at work was the norm.

1984

The percentage of married women working reached 50.3%. For the first time, it surpassed the rate of housewives.

1993

Parental vacation: men and women have 1 year of leave to take care of their child.

1999

The pill was introduced. However, only 1.2% of women take the pill. Women who take it are considered to be fickle.

2002

Underground cars reserved for women were established in Osaka for the first time.

Glossary

Biological attributes:
Properties belonging to a living organism.
Menstruation :
Periodic blood flow in females, more usually called "the period"
Meiji Restoration :
A period of changes for Japan, enormous changes in its politics and its social structure happened. It marks the end of the Japanese feudal society to become a society of industrialisation close to the occidental’s systems.
Federation of Women's Organisations:
In 1923, hundreds of women leaders from different organisations came together to form this organisation. Later, they created a section dedicated to women's right to vote and they fought for it.
